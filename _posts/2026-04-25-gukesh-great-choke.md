## "The era where World Champions were the best players in the world died when Carlsen gave up his throne." - Garry Kasparov

I personally was not very shocked to see the superstar on fire defeat the inactive, tired player who was a mere shell of his former self who had indeed stated himself that he felt weary and practically happier after his loss, but it was an interesting moment for the chess world where the vacuum left by Carlsen had been taken by the younger generation. This was much to the chagrin of one Kasparov, who to me provided a very amusing 10 minutes ranting about the shift of chess to a younger generation. But aside from some of his ranting, which to be frank, was quite justified, he made some excellent points. Besides pointing out the obvious, his views on FIDE world champions were simply that it was just a 'FIDE title' and that whoever owned the title at the time was not necessarily the best player in the world, far from it. Even before Carlsen, the level of dominance Anand, and before him Kasparov (although perhaps Karpov would disgaree), and before him Fischer, all the way back to Capablanca and Morphy, displayed was simply above their peers. The only such player alive today is clearly Carlsen himself - what happens to the throne after he abdicated?
After Ding's infamous Rg6 to secure the World Championship in 2023, he virtually disappeared from competitive chess (although his own story is truly one of incredible outcomes, maybe I'll cover it), leaving many people convinced that the new challenger would become the new FIDE title holder. Indeed, his win in the Chennai Grand Masters with a [conservative last round draw against Harikrishna](https://lichess.org/broadcast/chennai-grand-masters-2023/round-7/ZklyKUjQ/EfVc2ylZ) pretty much guaranteed him to be a threat in the future. It also spoke to how the chess world was indeed getting younger, as Erigaisi would finish second only on tiebreak. After qualifying for the Candidates, the old Americans were still clear favorites, 'but okay, he had good chances to win'. I did not find Gukesh to be a threat until his rather impressive Round 5 win against Abasov
